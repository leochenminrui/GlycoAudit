\subsection{Biological case studies and failure mode analysis}

To illustrate benchmark behavior and interpretability, we examine three representative agents:

\textbf{Case 1: AB_CB} (5 positives). Nearest-neighbor retrieval achieves MRR=0.457, substantially outperforming prototype-based ranking (MRR=0.360). Structural profile analysis reveals terminal proxy as the most distinctive feature (z-score=0.77), indicating moderate structural preference. Strong nearest-neighbor performance suggests well-defined structural binding determinants captured by SSV features. \n\n\textbf{Case 2: CG_GL_000782} (5 positives). Nearest-neighbor retrieval achieves MRR=0.457, substantially outperforming prototype-based ranking (MRR=0.279). Structural profile analysis reveals radius of gyration as the most distinctive feature (z-score=1.83), indicating this agent preferentially binds larger/more complex glycans. Strong nearest-neighbor performance suggests well-defined structural binding determinants captured by SSV features. \n\nThese case studies demonstrate that SSV-based nearest-neighbor retrieval can recover interpretable structural preferences when sufficient positive examples exist. Performance limitations highlight benchmark challenges: sparse positive counts, potential multimodality, and the need for sequence-level motif recognition beyond geometric shape features.\n
